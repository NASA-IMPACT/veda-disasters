   ## About
   In January 2025, Southern California experienced a devastating series of wildfires that scorched over 57,000 acres across Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, San Diego, and Ventura counties. The most destructive were the Eaton Fire in Altadena and the Palisades Fire in Pacific Palisades, driven by hurricane-force Santa Ana winds and prolonged drought conditions. These fires resulted in at least 30 fatalities, displaced more than 200,000 residents, and destroyed or damaged over 18,000 structures, marking them among the most catastrophic in California's history .
   </Prose>
 </Block>


<Block>
  <Prose>
  ## Impact
  The impacts of these wildfires were profound and multifaceted. Economically, the region faced estimated property and capital losses ranging from $76 billion to $131 billion, with insured losses up to $45 billion . The fires also led to a 0.48% decline in Los Angeles County's GDP and a total wage loss of approximately $297 million. Environmentally, the fires degraded air quality and posed long-term threats to coastal and ocean health due to runoff and debris . Recovery efforts were further hampered by federal budget cuts, which strained disaster relief operations and delayed aid distribution . Innovations like the deployment of Heli-Hydrants offered some hope, enabling quicker water access for firefighting helicopters and improving response times .
  </Prose>
</Block>
